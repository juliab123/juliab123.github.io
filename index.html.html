A glance At My Life

Q & A


What is your favorite book?

My favorite book is Tuesdays With Morrie by Mitch Albom.
If you’ve never read it, I highly recommend that you do,
because, simply put, it will change your life. The author’s
former college professor, Morrie Swchartz, has been diagnosed
with the deadly ALS, and Mitch has come back to write about the
psychologist’s take on death and dying. It will also make you cry.

What is the food you like the best?

Well, that's a tough question, at least, it is for me.
I am absolutely in love with waffles, which, by the way,
I consider better than pancakes becuase you can put maple
syrup and berries and whipped cream on waffles and it will
stay there instead of simply sliding off. Hmmm... but as for
food groups, I would say the most important things are cheese and
chocolate (and for when you're feeling upset, icecream).

If you had the chance, where would you travel to?

If I had the money and the time, I would travel all around
Europe. I have always dreamed of visiting:
1. The Buckingham Palace in England
2. The Eiffel Tower in France
3. The Colosseum in Italy
4. The Parthenon in Greece
and all the other wonderful places in the same continent
with so much to see. If you ever meet me, I probably
will seem like I have no desires for anything at all, but
travel is one of my secret favorite things to fantasize about.

What or who inspires you every day?

Something that inspires me every day is the goodwill that is
still present in this world, the fact that people are still
willing to be nice to each other, to look after and take care
of those in need, to sacrifice their time and effort for the
greater good. No specific person can ever be used to describe
this force as a whole, so I cite the mortal virtue of humanity
as a whole instead. It is much more accurate that way.

Tell us something interesting about yourself.

One thing that you'd never expect to be true about me is that
my birthday is on the last day of the year, making me the
youngest out of all the other people in my age group, which
can be pretty surprising. Also, people find it hard to believe
that I write poems/books in my spare time, becuase (and I quote)
I "do not look like a writer," at least, not to them.

What do you regret doing or not doing?

I don't really think that there is anything that I regret that
I have already done, so I won't go into that. But as for things
I regret not doing, I regret not being more open about my feelings
to people around me, I regret not taking control of my life and
following others blindly in hopes that it might get me somewhere.
In the end, I regret so much, yet, at the same time, so little.

What advice would you give to a teenager?

Every teenager needs to own up to what they are really, truly
thinking, to what they really want. The moment they confess
their true desires, the moment they reveal their true selves,
the moment they let everyone see what is really behind the
mask, that is the moment they become an adult. Finally.
